Station 01 · 2010 – 2014 · Maxwell & Partners

Strategy
Consultant.

Learn the craft first — before you raise your own buildings.

Why · Motivation

The wish to work through complex problems with structure — and to learn at the highest level what strategic consulting really means.

What · Impact

Led four international M&A projects. Carried an 80-million carve-out for a consumer goods manufacturer to closing in 14 months.

Next · Step

Closer to execution. Consulting sets the structures — I wanted to build them myself.

Station 02 · 2014 – 2018 · Bromer Industries

Head of
Strategy.

From recommending to owning — a different discipline.

Why · Motivation

Building a strategy function in a mid-sized group that did not have one before. From consultant to owner.

What · Impact

Built the strategy team to eight people. Prepared three acquisitions, two of them successfully integrated. Established US market entry with an own subsidiary.

Next · Step

I had hit my ceiling below the executive board. The next step was operational ownership with my own P&L.

The process starts at five minutes — you tell the full story, record it, and send it to Katrin. Feedback comes back, you sharpen, as many loops as it takes. Then you condense down to three minutes. That is the actual goal: a story with enough hooks so the person across from you absolutely wants to hear more. The one-minute version comes as an optional extra — for elevator situations.
